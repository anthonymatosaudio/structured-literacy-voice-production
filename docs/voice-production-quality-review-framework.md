# Voice Production Quality Review Framework

This framework brings together the checks I used while producing the structured-literacy videos. It covers AI-generated narration, human-recorded dialogue, and voice tracks assembled from multiple takes or sources.

I do not apply every item to every revision. The review is scaled to the scope of the work, the stage of production, and the risks most likely to affect the listener. A short pickup may require only script, edit-point, level, and export checks, while a complete instructional video may require every review pass. Items that do not apply can be marked **N/A**.

The approved script, pronunciation guidance, client specifications, and delivery requirements always take priority.

# Pass 1: Content, Pronunciation, and Performance

## Script Accuracy

* [ ] Spoken content matches the approved script.
* [ ] No words, phrases, or sentences are missing, repeated, substituted, or added unintentionally.
* [ ] Numbers, abbreviations, acronyms, names, and specialized terms are spoken correctly.
* [ ] Script revisions made during production are reflected in the current written version.
* [ ] The correct take or generation has been used.

## Pronunciation and Linguistic Accuracy

* [ ] Pronunciation is accurate and appropriate for the intended audience.
* [ ] Key terms remain pronounced consistently across passages.
* [ ] Edits preserve consonants, vowels, word endings, syllabic stress, and overall intelligibility.
* [ ] Alternate spellings or script adaptations have not introduced unintended pronunciations.
* [ ] Instructional examples model the intended sound, word, or language feature accurately.

## Delivery and Interpretation

* [ ] Pacing and pause length are appropriate for the content and intended listener.
* [ ] Prosody and emphasis support the structure and meaning of the passage.
* [ ] Tone, energy, and vocal character remain appropriate and consistent.
* [ ] Sentence endings, questions, transitions, and lists sound intentional.
* [ ] The delivery sounds natural without becoming flat, exaggerated, or mechanically paced.
* [ ] The listener has enough time to process complex or unfamiliar information.

---

# Pass 2: Take Selection, Editing, and Continuity

## Performance Continuity

* [ ] Combined takes sound like one intentional performance.
* [ ] Tone, timbre, pacing, energy, and vocal character remain consistent across sources and passages.
* [ ] Alternate generations or recordings have been matched as closely as the source material allows.

## Edit Integrity

* [ ] Edit points do not truncate consonants, vowels, breaths, or word endings.
* [ ] Crossfades preserve natural articulation without smearing transients or consonants.
* [ ] Phrase-, word-, syllable-, and phoneme-level edits remain transparent.
* [ ] Pause and timing adjustments preserve natural speech rhythm.
* [ ] Volume automation and clip-gain changes remain smooth and unobtrusive.
* [ ] No clicks, pops, discontinuities, or abrupt waveform changes are audible at edit points.

## Breath and Mouth-Noise Control

* [ ] Distracting breaths, mouth clicks, lip noises, and related vocal artifacts are controlled.
* [ ] Necessary breaths are retained or reshaped to preserve natural phrasing and avoid abrupt silence.
* [ ] Cleanup preserves consonant detail, speech rhythm, and any consistent room or background character.

## AI-Generated Voice Review

* [ ] No generated glitches, warbles, dropouts, pitch jumps, or unstable resonances are audible.
* [ ] Speaker identity, accent, tone, pronunciation, and prosody remain consistent.
* [ ] Generated phrase boundaries do not produce clipped attacks, unnatural tails, or timing artifacts.
* [ ] Regenerated material blends naturally with previously selected material.
* [ ] Material that could not be corrected effectively through editing was regenerated, rewritten, or replaced rather than forced into the final track.

---

# Pass 3: Processing, Mix, and Technical Quality

## Processing

* [ ] Gain staging remains controlled throughout the signal path.
* [ ] Corrective EQ improves clarity without making the voice thin, harsh, or unnatural.
* [ ] Compression controls dynamics without flattening expression or exaggerating noise.
* [ ] De-essing reduces distracting sibilance without dulling articulation.
* [ ] Spectral repair and noise reduction do not introduce metallic, watery, or phase-like artifacts.
* [ ] Transient processing preserves intelligibility around consonants and word attacks.
* [ ] Processing remains consistent across edited takes and passages.

## Levels and Mix Translation

* [ ] Voice levels remain consistent across the complete deliverable.
* [ ] No clipping, overload, or unwanted distortion is present.
* [ ] Loudness and peak levels meet the project’s delivery requirements.
* [ ] Narration remains intelligible alongside music, ambience, effects, and dialogue.
* [ ] Supporting audio does not mask important speech or instructional detail.
* [ ] The mix remains clear at both normal and reduced listening levels.
* [ ] The deliverable has been spot-checked on a second appropriate playback system when practical.

---

# Pass 4: Instructional and Visual Alignment

*Apply this pass to instructional and audiovisual deliverables.*

* [ ] Narration supports the instructional objective and intended learner outcome.
* [ ] Spoken explanations, demonstrations, annotations, and visuals remain correctly aligned.
* [ ] Timing gives viewers enough time to read, observe, and process the relevant information.
* [ ] Pronunciation, pacing, and emphasis direct attention to the intended concept.
* [ ] Transitions between explanation, modeling, guided practice, and application remain clear.
* [ ] The voice production supports the lesson without drawing unnecessary attention to the technology or editing.

---

# Pass 5: Final Playback and Delivery

## Full-Length Review

* [ ] The complete deliverable has been reviewed from beginning to end using the final exported file.
* [ ] Issues affecting accuracy, intelligibility, continuity, synchronization, or delivery have been resolved.
* [ ] Known edit points, regenerated sections, and previously corrected areas have been spot-checked.
* [ ] No new problems were introduced during the final revision or export.

## Export Verification

* [ ] File format, codec, sample rate, bit depth, channel configuration, and resolution meet the delivery brief.
* [ ] File naming and version numbering are correct.
* [ ] The exported file opens, plays, seeks, and ends correctly.
* [ ] For audiovisual deliverables, audio remains synchronized throughout the export.
* [ ] No required audio, video, captions, metadata, or supporting files are missing.
* [ ] The correct version has been placed in the designated delivery location.
* [ ] A verified backup or archive copy has been retained.
